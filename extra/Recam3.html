Introduction

Canon has launched the EOS 60Da, a variant of its 60D DSLR tuned for astrophotography. The camera features a re-worked infra-red filter leaving the camera more sensitive to a specific emission frequency (656nm) of hydrogen, key to capturing images of features such as nebulae (gas clouds) in space. I've pushed my 20Da (predecessor to this one) into service a few times and didn't really notice anything particularly 'better'. The IR enhancement is in the range of Hydrogen Alpha rather than full spectrum, so I'd be surprised if it helped much for that application. One hint - regardless of the camera you're using, use an external flash (I use the 580EX) and you'll dramatically reduce the number of false hits. Sort of like red-eye, having the flash close to the plane of the lens generates a lot of straight-back reflection. On a side-by-side test with clean optics, on-camera flash showed dozens of 'images', off-camera showed none. I have captured a few anomalies using off-plane flash, but usually only one or two per image.
